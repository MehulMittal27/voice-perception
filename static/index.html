Practical speaking tests

The current model responds to paralinguistic audio cues and SenseVoice emotion tokens, not guaranteed sentiment of the words alone. Try the same neutral sentence with different delivery.

  • Calm: steady volume and pace, relaxed tone.
  • Anxious or hesitant: uneven pace, uncertain tone, filler sounds, slight tremble.
  • Breathy pause: speak a phrase, pause with an audible breath, then continue.
  • Sad or low energy: lower pitch, softer voice, slower pacing.
  • Angry or agitated: sharper attack, louder voice, faster clipped phrasing.

SenseVoice-Small emotion tokens can be limited or unstable on short microphone demos. If this machine mostly emits NEUTRAL or unknown emotion tokens, treat no-speech filtering, transcript, audio events, and hesitation from breath, cough, cry, or silence as the reliable signals.
